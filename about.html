HOME FILM PHILOSOPHY ABOUT ME

My name is An Thy, a highschool senior from Hanoi, Vietnam. I was born and raised in Hanoi, where every street carries a story — yet only a few carry women’s names.

“Girls in Hanoi” began as a curiosity: why do so few streets in my city bear women’s names and what stories hide behind the ones that do? But over time, it became something larger—an ongoing project about visibility, safety, and belonging. It’s part research, part archive, and part love letter to Hanoi’s women: those who built, walked, and still navigate the city in quiet strength. I walk the same roads my grandmother once sold bánh cuốn on, the same roads built by women whose names never reached a street sign. Between the noise of motorbikes and the dusk-colored air, I look for the women who shaped this place — not always seen, but always present. In this project, I blend data, photography, and narrative to map not only where women are remembered, but how they exist today. Each page on this site holds a fragment of that story. I don’t claim to represent every woman’s experience, but I hope this project invites you to look closer to the street signs you pass, the corners you overlook, and the everyday resilience that fills them.

This is my Hanoi, told through the eyes of its girls, past and present.
